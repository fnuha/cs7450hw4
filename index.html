Manga has spanned a large variety of genres across the decades, reflecting the tastes of readers.

Let's take a look at the ways its makeup has changed over time.

In the year 1990, comedy, drama and action dominated the scene. This reflects the saturday-morning-cartoon origins of the genre, aimed more towards younger kids than adults.

As the years progress, romance and drama both became more popular, as did shoujo (manga targeted at young girls), reflecting a more diverse audience.

As times shifted, LGBTQ romance genres such as Boys' Love also became more popular.

As of recent, fantasy has been growing...

... to become the most popular genre for newly published manga in 2020.

As shown prior, romance, comedy, fantasy and action have tended to dominate when it comes to new publications.

Despite that, they end up middling in terms of average score, perhaps due to oversaturation of the genre.

However, despite the increasing quantity of manga published each year, average ratings from readers in recent decades have improved and stabilized from the early days of manga!

This stabilization may actually be because of the quantity of newly published manga, as recent years show a larger range of scores amongst publications, which may have balanced fluctuations in average scores per year overall.

Despite it all, the larger range of scores indicates the overall growth of the manga industry, as more manga authors are given greenlights to pursue their series and explore new ideas. More diverse titles can only mean good things for a creative industry.
